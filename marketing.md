# CivicLens — Business & Go-to-Market

> The business case for CivicLens and how we sell it. Companion to `product-todo-list.md` (what we build) and `absolute-docs/pdd.md` (why it exists).

---

## 1. The One-Liner

**CivicLens gives small business owners the regulatory intelligence that used to require a $500/hr lawyer** — turning fragmented government data into personalized, plain-English compliance guidance with citations, cross-city comparison, and ongoing monitoring.

**The sharper version (post Phase-1 actionability):**
> We don't just tell you which rules apply — we tell you *what to do, who to contact, and what to say* to stay compliant. A compliance command center for small businesses.

---

## 2. The Problem (why anyone pays)

In Texas, there is **no single place** to find what rules apply to your business. Obligations are scattered across state agencies, counties, cities, and industry boards that don't coordinate. The cost of getting it wrong:

| Pain | Cost |
|------|------|
| Fines for unknown violations | $500 – $5,000+ |
| Failed inspections / delayed openings | Days–weeks of lost revenue |
| Permit denials (zoning discovered too late) | Deal-breaking |
| Manual research time | 40–80 hours per business |
| Legal consultation | $200–$500/hour, often unaffordable |

**The wedge:** businesses *with* lawyers get this mapped in an afternoon. Businesses *without* spend weeks and still miss things. CivicLens closes that gap.

## 3. Who We Sell To

### Primary (MVP focus)
Small & mid-sized Texas business owners (1–50 employees) in high-regulation industries who **can't afford consultants** and are **new, expanding, or recently penalized**:
- Restaurants / food service / food trucks ← **beachhead**
- Retail (incl. e-commerce) · Salons / cosmetology · Construction contractors · Childcare

### Why food service first
Obvious regulatory complexity (health + alcohol + zoning + tax), high stakes, strong willingness to pay, and **organized distribution channels** (restaurant associations) to reach them.

### Highest-intent moment
The buyer is most motivated **before opening or expanding** — signing a lease, adding alcohol, moving cities. That's when "what could go wrong?" is worth real money to answer.

## 4. How We Make Money

A freemium wedge into recurring monitoring revenue.

| Tier | Price (target) | What's included | Why it converts |
|------|----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Free Basic Scan** | $0 | One profile + one risk scan with citations | Zero-friction lead gen; proves value instantly |
| **One-Time Expansion Report** | ~$49–99 | City-to-city or new-service-line readiness report (PDF) | Pay-once before a lease/expansion decision |
| **Pro / Monitoring** | ~$10–20 / mo | Saved profiles, alerts, deadline & renewal tracking, ongoing monitoring | Recurring value = the subscription wedge |
| **Multi-Location** | Higher tier | Franchises, chains, multi-city operators | More complexity, higher willingness to pay |

**Why monitoring is the real business:** a one-time scan is a transaction; **deadlines, renewals, and rule-change alerts are a reason to come back every month.** Recurring monitoring is the clearest path to durable revenue.

**Lead capture:** require an email to save, export, or monitor a report — converts demo/scan interest into a follow-up pipeline.

## 5. Go-to-Market: How We Reach Them

Small businesses are expensive to reach one-by-one. We grow through **trusted intermediaries** who already serve this audience.

| Channel | Why it works | Motion |
|---------|--------------|--------|
| **Restaurant associations** | Organized, our beachhead vertical | Channel partnership / member benefit |
| **Chambers of Commerce** | Already serve local SMBs; trusted | White-label / co-branded portal |
| **SBDCs (Small Business Development Centers)** | Trusted by entrepreneurs; distribution channel | Package as an advisor tool |
| **Accountants & bookkeepers** | Already advise SMBs on compliance-adjacent matters | Referral / report-generation mode |
| **Permit consultants** | Want faster research + client intake | Pro tier for professionals |

**Sequencing:** land **food service via restaurant associations + chambers** first → expand verticals (salons, contractors, childcare) → broaden Texas city coverage. Direct self-serve (free scan → paid) runs underneath all of it.

## 6. Why We Win (Positioning & Moat)

**Positioning:** Not a lawyer, not a Google search, not an AI lobbyist. **The compliance command center for small businesses** — lead with existing-rule compliance, then retain with monitoring.

**Differentiators that are hard to copy:**
1. **Citations are non-negotiable.** Every finding links to its source; uncited findings are never shown. Trust *is* the product — and it's what separates us from "ChatGPT with a wrapper."
2. **The diff engine.** City-to-city and old-vs-new rule comparison is far more defensible than generic regulatory search. It's the "wow."
3. **Actionability (Phase 1).** Impact scores + action playbooks + draftable agency emails turn information into execution. "We turned your rules into a plan."
4. **The dataset becomes the moat.** A verified, structured, freshness-tracked dataset of official Texas sources + an activity→permit ontology gets harder to replicate over time.

**Defensibility note:** the strict MVP is replicable; the *data asset, validated-scenario library, and outcome feedback loop* are not. Invest there to widen the moat.

## 7. The Pitch Narrative (demo + investor)

1. **Human story** — a real Texas owner hit by a fine / permit failure / late discovery. (Lock a real, specific story before any pitch — it's the credibility anchor.)
2. **The problem** — compliance is fragmented across agencies with no single lookup.
3. **Why now** — LLMs finally make unstructured government text queryable, with citations, in seconds.
4. **Live demo** — type a business → auto-classify → cited risk findings → click a citation → **impact score + action playbook + drafted agency email** → city-to-city diff → monitoring alert preview.
5. **Close** — *"CivicLens doesn't replace lawyers. It replaces the midnight Google session. Every owner deserves to know what rules apply before they get fined for breaking one."*

## 8. Metrics That Matter

**Product/trust:** citation accuracy (target 100% valid links), citation coverage %, finding accuracy on validated scenarios.
**Demo/credibility:** zero-crash demo runs, time-to-first-finding.
**Business:** free-scan → email-capture rate, scan → paid conversion, monitoring MRR & churn, expansion-report attach rate, channel-partner-sourced signups.

---

## 9. Strategic Guardrails (don't drift)

- **Stay a compliance tool, not a lobbying/political product** — that weakens the SMB identity.
- **Citations stay mandatory** — the moment we show an uncited finding, we're a liability, not a product.
- **Make every output actionable** — explaining rules is table stakes; telling owners what to *do* is the value.
- **Add a disclaimer everywhere findings appear** — "Informational guidance, not legal advice." Necessary risk control.
- **Lead with the diff engine and the Action Playbook** — they're the most differentiated, most demo-able, most valuable pieces.
