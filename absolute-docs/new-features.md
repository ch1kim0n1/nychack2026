# CivicLens — New Features to Add on Top of the PDD

Purpose: This document lists additional features that should be layered on top of the current CivicLens Product Development Document to make the product stronger, more defensible, more useful, and more investor/demo-ready.

---

## Highest-Priority Additions

- **Business Impact Score** — Score each compliance issue by money risk, delay risk, legal severity, urgency, and confidence.  
  **Why:** Owners do not care about abstract regulation; they care about whether it costs money, blocks opening, or creates fines.

- **Action Playbook** — For every finding, show exactly who to contact, what to ask, what documents to prepare, and what to do next.  
  **Why:** This turns CivicLens from an information tool into an execution tool.

- **Draft Assistant** — Generate emails, call scripts, permit questions, landlord questions, and agency inquiry messages.  
  **Why:** Small business owners often know they need help but do not know how to ask the right government office the right question.

- **Regulatory Threat Radar** — Monitor proposed bills, city ordinances, rule changes, fee changes, and agency updates relevant to a saved business profile.  
  **Why:** This adds the strongest “Fed10-like” value without turning CivicLens into a lobbying product.

- **Compliance Pulse 2.0** — Upgrade the current email mockup into a personalized digest of new risks, deadlines, proposed changes, and required actions.  
  **Why:** Recurring monitoring creates a reason for users to come back and pay monthly.

- **Stakeholder Map** — Show which city, county, state, or agency department owns each compliance issue.  
  **Why:** The hardest part for owners is often figuring out who is responsible for the answer.

- **Old Rule vs New Rule Diff** — Compare current requirements against proposed or recently changed requirements.  
  **Why:** This extends the existing city-to-city diff engine into a time-based change detection engine.

- **Before-You-Sign-Lease Checklist** — Generate a checklist of zoning, alcohol, occupancy, signage, parking, and health permit risks before the owner signs a location lease.  
  **Why:** Many expensive compliance mistakes happen before the business even opens.

- **Expansion Readiness Report** — Produce a printable report showing whether a business is ready to expand into another city or service line.  
  **Why:** This gives users a concrete artifact they can share with partners, landlords, lenders, or advisors.

- **Permit Path Timeline** — Convert compliance steps into an estimated timeline from idea to opening.  
  **Why:** Time-to-open is a major business risk, especially for restaurants and food trucks.

## Product Intelligence Features

- **Business Profile Watchlist** — Save user business profiles and automatically watch for rule changes matching their city, industry, and activities.  
  **Why:** Persistent profiles turn CivicLens from a one-time search tool into ongoing compliance infrastructure.

- **Activity-Based Matching** — Match rules based on specific activities like alcohol service, outdoor seating, food prep, hiring employees, delivery, or childcare.  
  **Why:** Industry labels alone are too broad and miss the actual compliance triggers.

- **Expansion Scenario Builder** — Let users model “what if” scenarios like adding alcohol, moving cities, opening a second location, or adding nail services.  
  **Why:** CivicLens becomes a planning tool instead of just a reactive compliance lookup.

- **Compliance Risk Heatmap** — Display risks by jurisdiction, business activity, severity, and urgency.  
  **Why:** Visual clustering helps users understand where the real danger is.

- **Regulatory Dependency Graph** — Show which permits depend on other permits, approvals, inspections, or documents.  
  **Why:** Users need to know sequence, not just a flat list of requirements.

- **Hidden Requirement Detector** — Flag requirements that are easy to miss because they come from a different agency or jurisdiction.  
  **Why:** This is where CivicLens can create the strongest “I would have missed that” moment.

- **Business Change Detector** — Ask follow-up questions when a user’s plan changes in a way that could trigger new rules.  
  **Why:** Compliance risk often appears when an owner adds one detail, such as alcohol, employees, seating, or delivery.

- **Regulation-to-Action Translator** — Convert dense legal or government language into specific business tasks.  
  **Why:** Plain-English summarization is useful, but task extraction is more valuable.

- **Compliance Gap Analyzer** — Compare what the user has already done against what is still missing.  
  **Why:** This makes the product more practical for businesses already mid-process.

- **Confidence-Aware Findings** — Label findings as high, medium, or low confidence depending on source quality and citation strength.  
  **Why:** It makes the system more trustworthy and prevents overconfident hallucinations.

## Fed10-Inspired Features Adapted for CivicLens

- **Business-Specific Threat Detection** — Detect whether a proposed rule or recent update matters to a specific user profile.  
  **Why:** Generic regulatory alerts are noisy; profile-aware alerts are useful.

- **Affected Business Explanation** — Explain why a specific business is affected by a rule, not just what the rule says.  
  **Why:** This is the difference between search results and actual intelligence.

- **Recommended Response Path** — Suggest whether the user should monitor, contact an agency, update documents, change plans, or seek clarification.  
  **Why:** Users need decision guidance, not only information.

- **Who-To-Contact Cards** — For every risk, display the responsible department, agency, support organization, or local office.  
  **Why:** It borrows Fed10’s “who to call” value but keeps it non-political and compliance-focused.

- **What-To-Say Generator** — Generate a short script for talking to permit offices, city departments, landlords, or licensing agencies.  
  **Why:** Many owners lose time because they ask vague questions and get vague answers.

- **Rule Change Impact Summary** — Summarize what changed, who is affected, when it matters, and what action is needed.  
  **Why:** This gives users an immediate reason to care about regulatory updates.

- **Policy Watch Mode** — Let users follow a specific topic like alcohol service, outdoor seating, food trucks, sales tax, zoning, or childcare licensing.  
  **Why:** Users may not want every update; they want updates tied to their business model.

- **Proposed Ordinance Monitor** — Track city council agendas and proposed ordinances for relevant business-impacting changes.  
  **Why:** Local regulation often hits small businesses before they know it exists.

- **Agency Notice Monitor** — Watch agency pages for updates to licensing, permits, inspections, fees, or renewal rules.  
  **Why:** Many critical compliance changes never become headline news.

- **Regulatory Timeline Watch** — Track effective dates and deadlines attached to proposed or adopted rule changes.  
  **Why:** A rule that matters in six months should trigger preparation now.

## Workflow and Execution Features

- **Step-by-Step Compliance Checklist** — Turn each risk finding into an ordered checklist with status tracking.  
  **Why:** Checklists are more actionable than paragraphs.

- **Task Status Tracking** — Let users mark tasks as not started, in progress, submitted, approved, rejected, or blocked.  
  **Why:** Compliance is a process, not a single answer.

- **Document Requirement List** — Show all documents needed for each permit, license, or inspection step.  
  **Why:** Missing documents are a common reason for delays.

- **Deadline Tracker** — Track filing deadlines, renewal dates, inspection windows, and response deadlines.  
  **Why:** Deadlines create real business risk and recurring product value.

- **Renewal Reminder System** — Alert users before licenses, permits, insurance, tax registrations, or inspections need renewal.  
  **Why:** Renewals are simple to monitor and easy to monetize.

- **Opening-Day Readiness Checklist** — Generate a final pre-launch checklist before a restaurant, salon, store, or daycare opens.  
  **Why:** This creates a clear, high-stakes moment where the product feels essential.

- **Blocked Step Explainer** — Explain why a compliance task cannot move forward until another step is completed.  
  **Why:** Users need dependency clarity to avoid wasting time.

- **Permit Submission Packet Builder** — Assemble required forms, source links, document lists, and agency instructions into one packet.  
  **Why:** This creates a tangible deliverable beyond the dashboard.

- **Landlord Question Generator** — Generate questions to ask landlords about zoning, alcohol, occupancy, signage, parking, and renovations.  
  **Why:** Lease mistakes can be more expensive than permit mistakes.

- **Consultant Handoff Report** — Create a structured summary the owner can send to a lawyer, accountant, permit expediter, or advisor.  
  **Why:** CivicLens can reduce professional-service time instead of pretending to replace every expert.

## Trust, Safety, and Citation Features

- **Citation Verification Layer** — Automatically check whether every generated finding has a valid source URL and matching quoted evidence.  
  **Why:** Citation accuracy is one of the most important trust signals in this product.

- **Evidence Drawer** — Let users expand a finding to see the exact source excerpt behind the recommendation.  
  **Why:** Users and judges trust the product more when the evidence is visible.

- **No-Citation Suppression Rule** — Hide any answer that cannot be tied to a source.  
  **Why:** This prevents the product from becoming a risky legal hallucination machine.

- **Source Freshness Badge** — Show when each source was last checked and whether it may be stale.  
  **Why:** Regulatory information changes, and stale guidance is dangerous.

- **Jurisdiction Badge** — Clearly label whether each requirement is city, county, state, federal, or agency-specific.  
  **Why:** It helps users understand where each rule comes from.

- **Informational Guidance Banner** — Display a concise notice that CivicLens provides informational guidance, not legal advice.  
  **Why:** This is necessary risk control for a compliance product.

- **Contradiction Detector** — Flag when sources appear to conflict or when one agency page does not match another.  
  **Why:** Fragmented government data often creates ambiguity, and ambiguity should be surfaced, not hidden.

- **Manual Validation Mode** — Allow internal admins to manually approve demo-critical findings before release.  
  **Why:** This protects the MVP from embarrassing hallucinations during demos.

- **High-Risk Finding Lock** — Require higher confidence or manual review for findings involving alcohol, childcare, health, fines, or zoning.  
  **Why:** High-impact categories should not be treated like casual search results.

- **Source Priority Ranking** — Prefer official government sources over blogs, law firm pages, or third-party summaries.  
  **Why:** The product should be grounded in primary sources whenever possible.

## Data and Backend Features

- **Regulatory Source Registry** — Maintain a database of all sources with jurisdiction, agency, topic, URL, last checked date, and reliability level.  
  **Why:** Good source management becomes a defensible data asset.

- **Jurisdiction Tagging System** — Tag every chunk by city, county, state, agency, industry, and activity.  
  **Why:** Accurate retrieval depends on good metadata, not just embeddings.

- **Activity Tag Taxonomy** — Build standardized tags like alcohol_planned, outdoor_seating, food_preparation, delivery, childcare, nail_services, construction_work.  
  **Why:** Tags make matching cleaner, faster, and more explainable.

- **Regulatory Change Log** — Store previous versions of ingested sources to detect changes over time.  
  **Why:** Change detection is required for serious monitoring.

- **Source Diff Pipeline** — Compare newly scraped documents against prior versions and summarize meaningful changes.  
  **Why:** This enables “what changed?” alerts without manually reading everything.

- **City Council Agenda Parser** — Ingest city council agendas and identify items related to business permits, zoning, fees, food service, alcohol, or inspections.  
  **Why:** Local policy changes often appear in agendas before they become obvious.

- **Permit Fee Extractor** — Extract fee amounts and display estimated cost ranges.  
  **Why:** Cost visibility makes the dashboard more business-relevant.

- **Effective Date Extractor** — Extract dates when rules, fee changes, or ordinances become active.  
  **Why:** Timing is central to business planning.

- **RAG Query Audit Log** — Store what documents were retrieved and how each answer was generated.  
  **Why:** Debugging and compliance trust require traceability.

- **Citation Coverage Metric** — Track the percentage of findings with valid, source-backed evidence.  
  **Why:** This gives the team a measurable quality target.

## Frontend and UX Features

- **Risk Dashboard Cards** — Display each issue as a card with severity, impact, deadline, confidence, source, and next step.  
  **Why:** Cards are easier to scan than long generated reports.

- **Action Button Per Finding** — Add buttons like “Draft Email,” “View Source,” “Add Deadline,” “Mark Done,” and “Compare City.”  
  **Why:** Users should not have to figure out what to do after reading a finding.

- **Diff Viewer Upgrade** — Expand the current Dallas vs Austin comparison into a clean “same / changed / new / removed / unknown” table.  
  **Why:** This makes the differentiator immediately obvious.

- **Impact Labels** — Use labels like “Could delay opening,” “Could trigger fine,” “Must verify before lease,” or “Renewal risk.”  
  **Why:** Business-language labels are more useful than legal categories.

- **Guided Intake Follow-Ups** — Ask targeted follow-up questions only when needed, such as alcohol, seating, employees, delivery, or renovations.  
  **Why:** A natural-language form is good, but intelligent follow-up improves accuracy.

- **Plain-English Mode** — Show a simplified version of each rule for non-technical users.  
  **Why:** Many small business owners do not want legal text.

- **Detailed Evidence Mode** — Let advanced users expand the full legal/government source context.  
  **Why:** Some users need more detail before taking action.

- **Print/Export Report Button** — Export findings, checklist, citations, and action plan as a PDF or markdown report.  
  **Why:** Owners need to share compliance information with partners, landlords, and advisors.

- **Demo Mode Toggle** — Provide a locked, prevalidated scenario for judges and presentations.  
  **Why:** A flawless demo matters more than a fragile live system during hackathons.

- **Confidence Warning UI** — Visually separate verified findings from uncertain or incomplete findings.  
  **Why:** It prevents users from treating weak guidance as final truth.

## Recommended Build Order

- **Phase 1: Add Business Impact Score** — Add money, time, urgency, confidence, and operational risk to every finding.  
  **Why:** Highest value, lowest complexity.

- **Phase 1: Add Action Playbook** — Add who to contact, what to ask, documents needed, and next steps.  
  **Why:** Makes the dashboard immediately actionable.

- **Phase 1: Add Draft Assistant** — Generate agency emails and call scripts from each finding.  
  **Why:** Creates an easy demo wow moment.

- **Phase 1: Upgrade Diff Viewer** — Add same, changed, new, removed, and unknown categories.  
  **Why:** Strengthens the most unique MVP feature.

- **Phase 1: Add Demo-Safe Static Cache** — Preload the validated scenario and outputs.  
  **Why:** Protects the live demo from API or retrieval failure.

- **Phase 2: Add Compliance Pulse 2.0** — Turn the email mockup into personalized monitoring alerts.  
  **Why:** Creates recurring value.

- **Phase 2: Add Regulatory Threat Radar** — Monitor recent/proposed changes relevant to saved profiles.  
  **Why:** Brings the best Fed10-style feature into CivicLens.

- **Phase 2: Add Deadline and Renewal Tracker** — Track permits, renewals, inspections, and filing dates.  
  **Why:** Strong subscription driver.

- **Phase 3: Add Source Diff Pipeline** — Detect changes in government pages and documents over time.  
  **Why:** Required for real monitoring at scale.

- **Phase 3: Add Vertical Packs** — Build restaurant, salon, contractor, childcare, and retail modules.  
  **Why:** Focused vertical products are easier to sell and validate.

## Final Product Direction

- **Do not become an AI lobbyist product.**  
  **Why:** That would make CivicLens look like a smaller Fed10 and weaken the SMB compliance identity.

- **Become the compliance command center for small businesses.**  
  **Why:** This is clearer, more useful, more demo-friendly, and better aligned with the current PDD.

- **Lead with existing-rule compliance, then add monitoring.**  
  **Why:** Existing requirements create immediate pain; monitoring creates retention.

- **Start with food-service expansion across Texas cities.**  
  **Why:** Restaurants and food trucks have obvious regulatory complexity, strong demo value, and real willingness to pay.

- **Make every output actionable.**  
  **Why:** CivicLens should not just explain rules; it should tell owners what to do next.

- **Make citations non-negotiable.**  
  **Why:** Trust is the entire product.

- **Make the diff engine the centerpiece.**  
  **Why:** City-to-city and old-vs-new comparisons are more differentiated than generic regulatory search.

- **Make the Action Playbook the main user benefit.**  
  **Why:** The best product promise is not “we found rules”; it is “we turned rules into a plan.”
